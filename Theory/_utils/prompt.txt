- Mathematical formulation (objective function / optimality criterion)
- Training procedure (how parameters are estimated)
- Inference procedure (how the model is used for classification)

- Model assumptions
- Model variants and their differences
- Limitations of the approach

   Break the text into logical sections mapped to the axes above.

   - Details that are easy to forget or commonly missed

3. DEPENDENCY MAP
   In what order should I study the pillars and what must I master 
   before moving to the next one.

4. VERIFICATION QUESTIONS
   For each pillar, 1-2 questions formulated exactly as they would 
   appear in the exam, to be answered with the book closed.

5. FINAL SUMMARY CARD
   At the end, produce a structured card with the model name, 
   the axes covered and the key points for each one. This card 
   will be used in the next phase for cross-model comparison, 
   so it must be precise and consistent in format.
